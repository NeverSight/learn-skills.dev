Weather-driven commodity trading analysis framework. This skill must be used whenever the user asks about: the impact of weather or climate events on commodity prices (cold snaps, heatwaves, droughts, hurricanes, floods, blizzards); climate patterns driving commodity markets (ENSO/El Niño/La Niña, polar vortex, Arctic Oscillation AO, North Atlantic Oscillation NAO, MJO, PNA); weather-risk analysis for energy commodities like natural gas/crude oil/power; weather-driven supply-demand analysis for agricultural products (grains/soybeans/coffee/sugar/cotton); applying weather forecast models (GFS/ECMWF) in trading; seasonal weather trends and commodity trading strategies. When the user directly sends weather-related images (weather charts, model output maps, CPC outlook charts, hurricane track maps, satellite cloud images, temperature anomaly maps, drought monitor maps, MJO phase diagrams, etc.), this skill must also be used to interpret the image and provide commodity trading analysis. Even if the user does not explicitly say "weather analysis," as long as the question involves extreme weather shocks to supply-demand, market transmission of climate patterns, weather-driven trading strategies, or any weather/meteorological images are sent, this skill should be used.

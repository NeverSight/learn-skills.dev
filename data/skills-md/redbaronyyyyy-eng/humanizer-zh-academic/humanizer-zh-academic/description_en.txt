A dedicated skill for lowering the AIGC-detection rate of Chinese academic writing (papers, journal articles, theses, etc.).  
Based on real-essay rewriting experiments (AIGC rate dropped from >50% to 11%), it distills patterns and cross-references Wikipedia’s “Signs of AI writing” systematic taxonomy.  
It detects and repairs typical Chinese-AI writing patterns: theory-based openings, “this case confirms” cliché closings, neat parallelisms, templated problem statements, passive analytical filler, over-symmetrical structures, superfluous summarizing, vague attributions, padding phrases, generic positive conclusions, and overused AI high-frequency words.  
Use when the user asks to: lower AI rate, human polish, reduce AIGC, humanize, de-AI flavor, academic editing, thesis weight-loss, AIGC detection, AI-trace removal, lower thesis AI rate, erase AI-writing traces, or academic text humanization.
